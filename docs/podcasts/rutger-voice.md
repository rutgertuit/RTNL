# Rutger — podcast voice profile

Canonical reference for how **Rutger** speaks in the podcasts. Every episode
script (and the `/weekly` Routine) writes his lines to this. It is deliberately
**warmer and looser than his on-stage / presenter voice** — closer to two
experienced peers talking than to a keynote.

This sits under the site-wide [[feedback-dutch-modesty-voice]] rule: modest,
plain, no grandiose epithets, none of the retired terms ("Conductor of Change",
"Tuit Doctrine", "doctrine" framing). The YouTube line is fine as plain prose
("YouTube is tv, social, search and shopping") — never labelled a doctrine.

## 1 · Core demeanour
- **Expert peer, not lecturer.** Relaxed and authoritative at once; it should
  feel like a collaborative conversation between people who both know the field,
  not a top-down brief.
- **Accessible enthusiasm.** His energy lifts on the technical stuff — but he
  frames it as an open, forward-looking *creative toolkit*, not a boardroom slide.
- **Warmth + musical pacing.** Fluid rhythm; he moves easily between a dense
  point and a lighter aside. Dry humour and quick direct asides break up the
  density. The occasional self-deprecating beat is fine; self-promotion is not.

## 2 · Rhetorical habits (use, don't overuse)
- **Sensory reframes.** Grounds an abstract shift in a simple physical image.
  Canonical example: *navigating modern marketing is like walking into a rented
  holiday kitchen — the cooking hasn't changed, only the drawers and the oven.*
- **Active demystification.** Meets hype/buzzwords by dismantling them and
  pivoting to the pragmatic question: *what does this actually change for the
  human on the other end? Where's the durable edge?*
- **Rule of three.** Packages a messy idea into a clean triad —
  *"measurement, personalization, content"*, *"hook, point, context"*. Makes a
  spoken point land and stick.

## 3 · Content instincts
- **Pragmatism over hype.** Steers away from vanity metrics and short-term
  trends toward what actually works in execution.
- **Human-centric.** Anchors data/AI points back to real behaviour (how a real
  team works on a Tuesday, what a viewer actually does).

## 4 · In scripts — cheat sheet
- Keep his turns conversational and not too long; let guests spar.
- One sensory analogy or one rule-of-three per episode is plenty — not every line.
- He can concede gracefully and still be the one who reframes the problem.
- **Light touch:** this profile updates his *register*; it does **not** mean
  taking the guests' wit away or flipping the straight-man dynamic. Guests still
  carry a lot of the comedy.
- End the episode on a real button (a callback or a clean landing), never a flat
  "thanks, that's it".
- Real punctuation only — no HTML entities (`&apos;` etc.); they break TTS.
- v3 audio tags ([laughs], [interrupting], em-dash cut-offs, … trailing) flow
  through to the model — use them sparingly and naturally.
